A goal to cultivate data-driven critical thought

Many of our society's debates at a relative's dinner table, within Twitter's No Man's Land, or elsewhere are based on anecdotes—of which there might be hundreds in the world to choose from—that in some cases are misleading or extreme representations of a topic. We aren't sufficiently informed by data or statistics; and though everyone should have concern for this personal responsibility, it isn't entirely our fault. Not only do people who are busy exploring passions or sometimes barely making a living have no patience or time to explore the dense and jargon-ridden hassles that primary sources can be... but the foremost media outlets, who feed off of our attention, emotions, and money, seemingly directly contradict each other every other day. So as to not waste our time arguing about the things of which we know quite little, and to furthermore evade the toxic manipulation by agenda-backed misinformers, the efficent and unbiased presentation of data is a must.

The purpose of this site is to comprehensively yet concisely visualize COVID (for now) information in real time, so that visitors can educate themselves and develop a relatively self-procured intuition. This goal will be accomplished through the laying out of figures, useful visuals, and maybe even particular claims/skepticism—which may or may not be well-grounded/accepted—in effect covering the idealogical gamut by logically reasoning through ideas and related stats.
